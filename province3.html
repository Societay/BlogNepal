Province:3 (Bagmati Pradesh)

The beauty and the known places of Province 3:

From magical mountains to scenic hills and wetlands of Tarai Nepal is famous not only for its diverse culture and rich natural heritage, but it also has diverse geographical regions, which makes it a must-visit place once in a lifetime. The newly-carved out Province 1 under the federal system is one of the seven provinces in the country. It has 12 districts spread from Mount Everest, the highest mountain in the world, to one of the greenest districts in Ilam and downward to Koshi Tappu, the serene wetland known for its wildlife reserve. Besides these natural wonders, this province has people from different ethnicities, making it an amalgamation of diverse culture and language.


How to reach Province 3:

The fastest way to travel is to take a direct flight to Kathmandu from your destination. It takes about 40 minutes (to Biratnagar) to 55 minutes (to Bhadrapur). You can also take a public bus which takes around 15 hours to reach Biratnagar and more time onwards, while you can also hire private vehicles.


Major Attractions of Province 3:
1. Patan Durbar Square:

Patan Durbar Square is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the city of Lalitpur, Nepal. It is a historic square that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region.Patan Durbar Square covers an area of approximately 7 hectares (17 acres). The square is home to numerous temples, palaces, and courtyards, adorned with intricate wood and stone carvings. The main attractions include the Krishna Mandir, Bhimsen Temple, and the Golden Temple. Visitors can explore the square, admire the architectural beauty, and learn about the history and culture of the area.


Best time to travel:
The best time to visit Patan Durbar Square is during the dry season, which is from October to April.

How to reach:

To reach Patan Durbar Square, you can take a taxi or a local bus from Kathmandu. It's located in Lalitpur, which is about 5 kilometers south of Kathmandu.


2. Pashupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath Temple is one of the most sacred Hindu temples in Nepal. It is located in Kathmandu, near the banks of the Bagmati River.This temple was classified as a World Heritage Site in 1979. It is built on an area of 246 hectares (2,460,000 m2) and includes 518 mini-temples[citation needed] and a main pagoda house. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered a significant pilgrimage site. The temple complex is known for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and religious rituals. It is also the site of the famous Pashupatinath Festival, which attracts thousands of devotees each year. It is a spiritual and cultural landmark that showcases the rich heritage of Nepal.



Best time to travel:
The best time to visit Pashupatinath Temple is during the Maha Shivaratri festival, which usually falls between February and March.However, the temple is open year-round and can be visited at any time.

How to reach:

The distance between Pashupatinath Temple and Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu is approximately 2.5 kilometers. It takes around 15-20 minutes by car to travel between the two.



3. Gosaikunda:


Gosaikunda Lake is a sacred alpine freshwater lake located in the Langtang National Park of Bagmati Province, Nepal. It is situated at an altitude of about 4,380 meters (14,370 feet) above sea level. The lake is surrounded by stunning Himalayan peaks and is considered a significant pilgrimage site for both Hindus and Buddhists. It is believed to be the abode of Lord Shiva. The lake is also known for its natural beauty and is a popular trekking destination in Nepal.


Best time to travel:

The best time to visit Gosaikunda Lake is during the months of May to October. This is the peak trekking season in Nepal when the weather is generally clear and the trails are more accessible.
